Economics · Policy

Data Sources for Economics and Policy: Federal, State, and Local

A curated collection of databases covering U.S. economic conditions, labor markets, business data, and policy indicators — at national, state, and local levels.

Subscription / Library Access
StatsUSA — Nearly 200 databases covering all 50 states, with ~80 detailed California databases. Data at national, state, and local levels.
SimplyAnalytics — Custom maps and downloadable U.S. datasets on demographics, consumer spending, crime, businesses, and weather. Available by state, county, zip code, census tract, and more.
RMA Statement Studies (via OneSearch) — Financial ratio benchmarks and composite data for U.S. companies by industry (NAICS). Search by size, region, and data type.
Wharton Research Data Services (WRDS) — Access to AuditAnalytics, Compustat, CRSP, Federal Reserve Bank data, and more. Free account registration required.
ABI/INFORM Complete (via OneSearch) — Business news, peer-reviewed research, and country industry reports. Part of ProQuest Databases.
CountryWatch — Country profiles, news, and political and economic statistics and trends.
Federal Government
Data.gov — Open repository of U.S. government public data, including geospatial datasets.
data.census.gov — Data on people, housing, business, and industry across the U.S.
Bureau of Labor Statistics — Employment and labor statistics from the U.S. Department of Labor.
U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA) — National economic accounts, GDP, industry data, and regional economic statistics.
Survey of Current Business — Data and analysis from BEA on U.S. economic performance.
FRED (St. Louis Fed) — Hundreds of thousands of economic time series from national, international, and private sources.
FRASER (St. Louis Fed) — Digital library of U.S. economic, financial, and banking history, including Federal Reserve policy documents.
State of California
Economic Research Unit — CA Dept. of Finance — Economic forecasts and analyses for state and local agencies.
California Economic Indicators — Monthly and annual reports on employment, income, construction, inflation, trade, and Gross State Product.
CA Employment Development Department — California labor market data and employment statistics.
History of California Minimum Wage — Historical wage statistics from the CA Dept. of Industrial Relations.
Think Tanks & Non-Government
The Conference Board — Nonpartisan nonprofit specializing in economic indicators, sustainability, corporate governance, and social responsibility.
Surveys of Consumers — University of Michigan — Consumer sentiment data and economic expectations surveys.
Shadow Government Statistics — Critical analysis and alternative measures of U.S. economic data and reporting.
